ABOUT CBT

CBT Exam – Computer Based Test is an assessment test which Nurses should give if they wish for a career in UK. The newly introduced CBT is a two-part examination. You only get to take the test after authorization from Pearson Vue when candidates are through with UK NMC registration and verification from their home regulatory body. Part A of the exams consist of 15-mark numeracy questions whiles Part B is a 100-mark clinical multiple choice questions. The candidate has to pass both parts of the CBT before they can be accepted into the NMC register for nurses. If a candidate fails either part, they need only re-sit the part they have failed.

In a disciplined nation as UK, a skilled nurse or midwife is supposed to apply standard practices, knowledge and tenets of nursing and midwifery and the CBT is done to evaluate a precise understanding of your skills & knowledge in Nursing and midwifery.

CBT STRUCTURE FOR NURSES

The nursing CBT is made up of 15 numeracy questions for Part A where candidate provides his or her own answers after some calculations. Part B is a 100 multiple-choice questions. Candidates will be assessed under the following domains for part B:

  • Being an accountable professional
  • Promoting health and preventing ill health
  • Assessing needs and planning care
  • Providing and evaluating care
  • Leading and managing nursing care and working in teams
  • Improving safety and quality of care
  • Coordinating care

CBT STRUCTURE FOR MIDWIVES

The midwifery CBT is made up of 15 numeracy questions for Part A where candidate provides his or her own answers after some calculations. Part B is a 100 multiple-choice questions. Candidates will be assessed under the following domains for part B:

  • Being an accountable, autonomous, professional midwife
  • Safe and effective midwifery care: promoting and providing
  • Continuity of care and carer
  • Universal care for all women and newborn infants
  • Additional care for women and newborn infants with complications
  • Promoting excellence: the midwife as colleague, scholar and leader
  • The midwife as skilled practitioner
